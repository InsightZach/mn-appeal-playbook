# Worked Example — 2913 Drew Ave S, Minneapolis (Hennepin County)

The cautionary case. This parcel had the **strongest** triage angle of the batch — and got **no change** —
because it was filed as a mailed written appeal with no appraiser engagement, in a jurisdiction whose
policy is to decide written appeals without an inspection.

## Triage

**Verdict: appeal angle — and a strong one.**

- **Subject's own sale below EMV** — purchased arm's-length for **$679,900 on 2024-08-01**, which is
  **8.6% below** the 2026 EMV of $744,200. The assessment never reset to the owner's own recent purchase.
- **Equalization** — building at **$267/SF vs. a $205/SF median (96th percentile)**; land mid-pack.

This is close to the best evidence an appeal can have: the property itself sold, at arm's length, well
below what the city now says it is worth.

## How it was run — and why it failed

The appeal was **mailed to the Local Board as a written appeal**, with no contact with a city appraiser
and no inspection. Per the City of Minneapolis Assessing director, on written appeals to the Minneapolis
Local Board the assessing department **presents no information and conducts no inspection** — it is city
policy. The board therefore heard the appeal as filed, with the department's position unchanged.

## Outcome

**No change.** The Local Board sustained the $744,200 value on all such appeals; the parcel was not
carried to the Hennepin County Board. The strongest angle in the batch produced nothing — not because the
evidence was weak, but because the **process skipped every point of engagement**: no open-book
conversation, no appraiser review, no inspection to put the $679,900 purchase in front of someone who
could act on it.

## The lesson

A mailed written appeal in a no-inspection jurisdiction is heard as filed. Even decisive evidence needs a
person to deliver it and a procedural path that lets someone act on it. For a Minneapolis parcel this
means engaging the appraiser *before* the Local Board, or — when a written appeal is sustained — planning
straight for the avenues where an independent review is possible (the County Board, or a Tax Court
petition by April 30 of the payable year). See [Submission](../docs/06-submission.md) and
[Jurisdiction Map](../docs/02-jurisdiction-map.md).
