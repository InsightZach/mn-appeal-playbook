# 1. The Appeal Calendar

Minnesota assessment appeals are governed by a fixed annual calendar. Missing a window does not just
weaken an appeal — it removes options entirely. The single most common, most expensive mistake is
treating the appeal as a document you file whenever it is ready. It is not. It is a sequence of dated
windows, and the highest-value window opens first.

## The two-year cycle

Minnesota separates the **assessment year** from the **payable year**.

| Notation | Value set (effective date) | Taxes payable |
|----------|---------------------------|---------------|
| 26p27 | January 2, **2026** | **2027** |
| 27p28 | January 2, **2027** | **2028** |

Every valuation input — comparable sales, condition, market conditions — is judged as of **January 2 of
the assessment year**. A sale that closed in March of the assessment year is *after* the effective date
and carries less weight than one from the prior fall.

## The annual rhythm

Exact dates print on each property's valuation notice and shift by a few days each year and jurisdiction.
The rhythm does not change:

| When | Event | What you do | Leverage |
|------|-------|-------------|----------|
| **~mid-to-late March** | Valuation notices mailed (value as of Jan 2) | Ingest the notice. Confirm the new assessment-year value. Run the over-assessment triage. | — |
| **Late March – April** | **Open Book / informal review** | Call the assessor. Request a review. Schedule the inspection. The appraiser can adjust the value unilaterally here. | **Highest** |
| **April (into May)** | **Local Board of Appeal & Equalization (LBAE)** | Appear before the local board — or attend Open Book where the jurisdiction has transferred LBAE duties to it. | Moderate |
| **Late April – early May** | Written-appeal / county-board request deadlines | Submit the request to be heard at the county board. Date is on the notice (Ramsey, for example, runs an early-May postmark deadline). | — |
| **After the 2nd Friday in June (~Jun 15–26)** | **County Board of Appeal & Equalization (CBAE)** | Present evidence to the county board. You must have gone through the local board / open book first. | Moderate |
| **April 30 of the payable year** | **Minnesota Tax Court petition deadline** | File a petition. This is the real backstop and carries the most leverage. | **Highest (formal)** |

> **Rule:** you must appear at the **local board (or open book) before** you can be heard at the
> **county board**. Skipping the first level forecloses the second.

### Statutory date windows

The exact dates print on each year's valuation notice, but the windows are set by statute and do not
move much:

| Stage | Statutory / customary window | Authority |
|-------|------------------------------|-----------|
| Valuation notices mailed | ~mid-to-late March (at least 10 days before the local board) | Minn. Stat. § 273.121 |
| Open Book review (transferred jurisdictions) | **April–May** | Minn. Stat. § 274.014 |
| **Local Board** of Appeal & Equalization | meets **between April 1 and May 31** | Minn. Stat. § 274.01 |
| **County Board** of Appeal & Equalization | convenes in **June** (Hennepin: mid-June; Ramsey: the special board meets after the **2nd Friday in June**, up to 10 consecutive meeting days) | Minn. Stat. §§ 274.13–274.14 |
| Request to be heard at the County Board | by the date on the valuation notice (often late April / early May) | per county |
| Tax Court petition | **April 30 of the payable year** | Minn. Stat. § 278.01 |

> Local board dates are jurisdiction-specific (each city/township sets its own meeting date within the
> April 1–May 31 window). County board dates are county-wide. Read the notice for the parcel's exact
> local dates, and confirm the county board schedule from the county assessor.

## Why the order matters

The windows are sequenced from *most* discretion to *most* formality:

- **Open book** is where an appraiser can simply agree you are right and change the number. It costs the
  county nothing and creates no record to defend. It is the cheapest reduction available — and the only
  one that happens *before* anyone has dug in.
- **The boards** (local, then county) are progressively more formal. They build a record but defer
  heavily to assessor staff. By the time you reach them, the staff position is usually set.
- **Tax Court** (petition by April 30 of the payable year) is the formal backstop with real leverage,
  because the county now faces litigation cost. Most petitions settle by stipulation. A stipulated value
  is binding for the year and carries weight into the next year — unlike an open-book concession, which
  the county can quietly reverse. (See [Negotiation & Escalation](08-negotiation-escalation.md).)

**The implication for operations:** start at the notice and work *backward* from each deadline. If you
arrive at the end of the cycle with a packet and no prior engagement, you have skipped every discretionary
window and left only the formal ones — where you are least likely to move the number this year.

## 26p27 — the worked example

| Milestone | 26p27 actual |
|-----------|--------------|
| Effective date | January 2, 2026 |
| Notices mailed | mid-March 2026 |
| Open book / local boards | late March – May 2026 |
| County boards convened | ~June 15, 2026 |
| Tax Court petition deadline | **April 30, 2027** |

## 27p28 — the live run

| Milestone | 27p28 projected |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Effective date | January 2, 2027 |
| Notices mailed | ~mid-to-late March 2027 |
| Open book / local boards | late March – May 2027 |
| County boards convened | ~mid-to-late June 2027 |
| Tax Court petition deadline | **April 30, 2028** |

> Confirm each year's exact dates from the valuation notice and the county assessor's published schedule.
> Build the operating calendar in February, before notices land, so the team is ready to engage the day
> a notice arrives — not weeks later.

## Working backward: the operating calendar

| Lead time before notices | Task |
|--------------------------|------|
| **Off-season (Aug–Feb)** | Build the parcel/sales/assessment data foundation per county. ([Data Sources](03-data-sources.md)) |
| **February** | Finalize the client list, confirm authorizations, draft the jurisdiction calendar. |
| **Notice day (mid-March)** | Ingest notices, run triage, decide which parcels are worth appealing. ([Triage](04-triage-decision.md)) |
| **Within days of the notice** | Generate packets for the appeal-worthy parcels; open the conversation with the assessor. ([Packet](05-packet-generation.md), [Submission](06-submission.md)) |
| **Late March – April** | Open book engagement + inspections. ([Inspections](07-inspections.md)) |
| **April – June** | Negotiate; escalate to county board where warranted. ([Negotiation & Escalation](08-negotiation-escalation.md)) |
| **By April 30 of payable year** | File Tax Court petitions on the strong cases that did not resolve. |
